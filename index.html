Could NIS2 apply to your company?

Machinery manufacturing is a listed sector under the German BSI Act.

The manufacture of machinery and equipment is listed in Annex 2 of the German BSI Act as a sector for important entities.5

A company carrying out a listed machinery-manufacturing activity may fall within scope if it employs at least 50 people, or if both annual turnover and annual balance-sheet total exceed €10 million. Exact classification may also depend on entity and group calculations, specific activities and statutory exceptions.6

Companies must assess their individual status. An individual assessment is required before deciding what needs to be implemented.

More than a policy document

NIS2 is an ongoing operating requirement, not a one-time certificate.

Covered organisations must implement appropriate, proportionate and effective technical and organisational measures. These measures include the following areas.7

Risk management

Policies and processes for analysing cybersecurity risk and protecting network and information systems.

Incident handling

Defined processes for detecting, escalating, responding to and documenting security incidents.

Business continuity

Backup management, disaster recovery and crisis-management arrangements.

Supply-chain security

Consideration of risks connected to suppliers, service providers and direct business relationships.

Vulnerability management

Processes for identifying, assessing, disclosing and remediating vulnerabilities.

People and access

Cybersecurity training, access controls, asset management, secure authentication and appropriate use of encryption.

Control effectiveness

Processes for assessing whether cybersecurity measures are operating effectively, not merely whether they have been documented.

The challenge is not simply knowing that these requirements exist. It is assigning ownership, implementing them across the organisation and keeping the supporting evidence current.

Preparation matters before an incident

Twenty-four hours is not the time to design your response process.

A significant cybersecurity incident may trigger staged reporting obligations.3

24 hours

Early warning

72 hours

Incident notification

Within one month after the 72-hour notification

Final report, generally

If the incident is still ongoing, a progress report may be required first, followed by the final report after the incident has been handled.

Meeting these deadlines requires clear detection and escalation criteria, named decision-makers, reliable technical information, documented actions and an established communication process.

The process must exist before it is needed.
